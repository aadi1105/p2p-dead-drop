Why 16KB is the Chosen Default

Although modern browsers support WebRTC messages up to 64KB or 256KB, the WebRTC SCTP layer implements message bundling and fragmentation. Sending blocks larger than 16KB can exhaust the browser's SCTP buffer queue on slower lines, causing head-of-line blocking or triggering congestion control window drops that cause throughput collapse. Under FIPS/standards-conforming WebRTC, a 16KB chunk size is the maximum safe block size that avoids application-level fragment collisions while maximizing stability across mobile and desktop clients.
